Sector coverage and practical relevance

Energy and Agriculture are primary because price formation is tightly linked to physical systems. Secondary sectors are covered where they alter commodity flow or realized pricing.

Energy (Primary)

Energy systems remain central because storage constraints, transport optionality, and refinery or generation bottlenecks directly influence price behavior and route economics.

Agriculture (Primary)

Agricultural supply chains are core due to weather variability, planting and harvest cadence, processing capacity, and export corridor constraints that shape seasonal imbalance.

Metals (Secondary)

Metals coverage is selective and tied to industrial demand, inventory location, and cost-curve sensitivity when those variables create clear trade-route exposure.

Logistics (Secondary)

Logistics is relevant where freight availability, vessel or rail constraints, and terminal access determine basis spreads and delivery reliability.

Infrastructure (Secondary)

Infrastructure optionality matters when storage, transfer, and processing assets can absorb shock and improve resilience within commodity corridors.

Operating Partnerships (Secondary)

Operating partnerships are considered where local execution quality, governance standards, and verified commercial alignment support sustained participation.
