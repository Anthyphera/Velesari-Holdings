Markets

Sector priorities and trade-route relevance

Energy and Agriculture lead allocation attention. Secondary sectors are reviewed when they affect commodity movement, pricing realization, operating access, or settlement reliability.

Primary sectors

Energy

Primary coverage where storage utilization, pipeline and freight constraints, regional demand shifts, and delivery routes can be monitored directly.

Agriculture

Primary coverage where weather cycles, crop movement timing, processing capacity, export corridors, and freight availability shape basis and spread behavior.

Secondary sectors

Secondary sectors remain subordinate and are considered when they alter route economics, operating access, collateral quality, or exposure resilience.

Logistics

Freight, terminal capacity, and routing constraints are monitored where they determine physical delivery.

Metals

Coverage is selective when metals dynamics influence adjacent industrial commodity demand or financing conditions.

Infrastructure & Operating Partnerships

Assessed where asset access and local operating capability improve control, timing, or execution reliability.
