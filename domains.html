Markets

Sector priorities and trade-route relevance

Energy and Agriculture lead allocation attention. Secondary sectors are assessed for their effect on commodity movement, pricing realization, and execution reliability.

Primary sectors

Energy

Priority coverage where storage utilization, pipeline and freight constraints, and regional demand shifts drive observable dislocation.

Agriculture

Priority coverage where weather cycles, crop movement timing, processing capacity, and export corridors shape basis and spread behavior.

Secondary sectors

Secondary sectors remain subordinate and are considered when they alter route economics, operational access, or exposure resilience.

Logistics

Freight and terminal capacity are monitored where they constrain or unlock physical delivery.

Metals

Coverage is selective when metals dynamics influence adjacent industrial commodity corridors.

Infrastructure & Operating Partnerships

Assessed where asset access and local operating capability improve execution optionality.
