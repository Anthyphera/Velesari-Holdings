Strategy

Decision discipline for proprietary capital allocation

Each exposure follows a fixed operational sequence. Capital is not committed until assumptions, constraints, and invalidation conditions are recorded.

Operational sequence

1) Market structure

Screen supply-demand balance, inventory stress, and curve behavior to define the dislocation under review.

2) Physical constraints

Map storage limits, transport bottlenecks, and substitution routes to test persistence and route optionality.

3) Risk limits

Set sizing caps, concentration bounds, and invalidation thresholds before any execution instruction is issued.

4) Counterparty review

Validate legal terms, settlement reliability, and operating capacity before exposure is opened.

5) Execution discipline

Enter in tranches when required, monitor fills and slippage, and suspend activity when pre-defined limits are breached.

6) Portfolio supervision

Review live positions versus thesis conditions and reduce or exit when evidence invalidates the original setup.
