Strategy

From thesis to supervised exposure

The strategy framework defines how opportunities are screened, where risk is capped, and how exposure is adjusted when assumptions change.

1) Opportunity qualification

Each idea is screened for observable structure: supply-demand balance, inventory pressure points, curve behavior, and likely regime triggers.

2) Physical system mapping

Storage and transport bottlenecks, throughput limits, and route substitutions are mapped to test whether dislocation conditions can persist.

3) Risk limits and invalidation levels

Position size ceilings, invalidation levels, and scenario bounds are documented before execution so downside is defined in advance.

4) Counterparty review and execution controls

Counterparty review covers legal terms, settlement readiness, and operating reliability before capital is committed.

5) Portfolio supervision

Open exposure is reviewed against the original thesis, with reductions, exits, or redeployments triggered by evidence rather than narrative preference.
